What tests do you offer?

Complete range: Radiology (MRI, CT, X-Ray, Ultrasound), Pathology (Blood, Urine, Hormones, Vitamins), Cardiology (ECG, Echo, TMT), and Health Packages (30+ to 100+ test panels).

How long does an MRI scan take?

Typically 20-45 minutes depending on body part. Brain: 20-30 min | Spine: 30-40 min | Joints: 20-30 min | Full Body: 60+ min. You must stay still during the scan.

Is MRI painful?

No, MRI is completely painless. You'll hear some noise during the scan (we provide earplugs/headphones). No needles unless contrast dye is needed. You just need to lie still.

What should I wear for MRI/CT?

Wear comfortable clothing without metal (no zippers, buttons, underwired bras). We provide a hospital gown if needed. Remove all jewelry, watches, mobile phones, and accessories before entering.

Are MRI and CT scans safe?

MRI: Uses magnetic fields — no ionizing radiation, very safe. CT: Uses controlled radiation — safe when medically necessary. Benefits always outweigh minimal risks.

Can pregnant women take scans?

Always inform us about pregnancy. Ultrasound: Safe throughout pregnancy. MRI: Generally safe after first trimester (with doctor's advice). CT/X-Ray: Generally avoided unless absolutely necessary.

How do I prepare for blood tests?

Most fasting tests need 8-12 hours fasting (only plain water allowed). Non-fasting tests like CBC, Thyroid don't need preparation. We'll provide specific instructions when you book.

Can I take my medicines before tests?

Usually yes with a sip of water, but inform us about all medications during booking. Some medicines (blood thinners, steroids, thyroid medication) may affect specific test results.

What if I'm claustrophobic (afraid of closed spaces)?

Inform us beforehand. Our MRI has a wide bore design (less enclosed feeling). We provide music/headphones. Mild sedation is available if needed (with doctor's prescription). Our staff will guide you throughout.

How much blood is drawn for tests?

Typically 2-5 ml per test. Even for comprehensive packages with 50+ tests, total blood drawn rarely exceeds 15-20 ml — that's about 3-4 teaspoons. Completely safe for adults.

Can diabetic patients undergo fasting tests?

Yes! Book a morning slot (7-8 AM). Come early, get tested first, then have breakfast immediately. Always carry glucose tablets/snack. Inform our staff about your condition. They'll prioritize your sample.

Do you perform tests on children?

Yes! We have specially trained pediatric phlebotomists who are experienced with children. We use smaller butterfly needles and create a child-friendly, comfortable environment.

Is radiation from CT scan dangerous?

Our 128-slice CT scanner uses controlled, low-dose radiation — safe when medically necessary. Benefits of accurate diagnosis far outweigh the minimal radiation risk. Not recommended repeatedly without medical need.

Can I see the needle before sample collection?
